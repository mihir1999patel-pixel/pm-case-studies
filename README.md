PM Case Studies
A collection of real situations I've navigated — structured as mini case studies to demonstrate PM thinking: problem identification, stakeholder management, decision-making, and outcome tracking.
Each entry follows the STAR format: Situation → Task → Action → Result.
---
Case Study 001 — SaaS Onboarding Acceleration (peAR Technologies, 2025)
Role: Business Development Executive (SaaS)
Context: B2B clients onboarding onto CRM, loyalty, and payment platforms
Situation:
Multiple clients were experiencing delayed go-lives due to unclear requirement handoffs between sales and the implementation team. Clients were unsatisfied and escalation rates were rising.
Task:
As the person coordinating between clients, sales, and internal teams, I needed to reduce friction in the handoff process without a formal PM title or authority.
Action:
Created a simple requirement clarification checklist used during early sales calls
Set up a shared tracking log for onboarding milestones visible to all three teams
Established a weekly 15-minute sync between sales and implementation to pre-empt blockers
Result:
Reduced back-and-forth clarification emails by approximately 40%
Two client go-lives delivered ahead of schedule in the following quarter
Checklist was adopted as standard practice by the wider team
PM Skill Demonstrated: Stakeholder alignment, process documentation, delivery tracking

Case Study 002 — Team Operations Overhaul (O'Neill's Oxford, 2023–2025)
Role: Assistant Manager
Context: 15+ member hospitality team, high-volume service environment
Situation:
Inconsistent scheduling led to understaffing on peak days and overstaffing on quiet days, hurting both sales performance and staff morale.
Task:
Redesign the weekly scheduling process to align resource allocation with actual demand patterns.
Action:
Analysed 8 weeks of footfall and sales data to identify true peak periods
Rebuilt the scheduling template around data-driven shift patterns
Ran a 4-week pilot with daily tracking against targets
Result:
Weekly sales increased by approximately 10% over the pilot period
Staff overtime reduced, improving morale scores in team feedback
New schedule template adopted permanently by management
PM Skill Demonstrated: Data-driven decision making, resource planning, continuous improvement

Case Study 003 — Engineering Project Coordination (Mistify AI, Oxford)
Role: Project Engineer
Context: Dust-suppression product development — hardware prototype
Situation:
Prototype testing was revealing inconsistent performance results. Multiple vendors and internal engineers were involved, with no single coordination point.
Task:
Coordinate the testing-feedback-redesign loop across vendors, engineers, and the performance data pipeline.
Action:
Created a shared test log that captured input parameters, test results, and next actions after each run
Held brief post-test syncs to align on design changes before re-engaging vendors
Maintained a decision log so changes were traceable
Result:
Reduced iteration cycle time from ~2 weeks to ~1 week per round
Achieved consistent performance benchmarks within 4 iterations
Full test documentation handed to client as part of final deliverable
PM Skill Demonstrated: Cross-functional coordination, iterative delivery, documentation
